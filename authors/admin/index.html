Ben J. Ward

Postdoc Researcher and Software Developer

The Earlham Institute

I’m a postdoctoral researcher and software developer in the Assembly & Algorithms (the A-Team) group, led by Bernardo Clavijo at the Earlham Institute in Norwich, England.

Before arriving at The Earlham Institute, I earned my PhD in Population Genetics from The School of Environmental Sciences at The University of East Anglia, where I was supervised by Professor Cock van Oosterhout. My researched focused on the vital role recombination and hybridisation plays in the adaptive evolution of organisms to dynamic environments. I have studied Diatoms living in seasonally changing ocean regions, and rapidly adapting pathogens such as Oomycetes and Fungi.

Now I help the A-Team’s efforts in developing methods that provide quality haplotype-phased assemblies, and for analysing genome assembly graphs. My hope is that they will enable population genetecists to move beyond analysing SNPs based on re-sequencing against some reference. Instead I hope we will be able to track entire haplotypes through populations & time, in a manner that casts my mind back to the first chapter in Richard Dawkins’ The Selfish Gene.

A lot of my research requires me to code and I enjoy creating and developing tools. I co-founded and maintain the BioJulia project, which develops and maintains software packages that fulfil many bioinformatics needs. The best software for me isn’t only fast and functional, but introduces or presents concepts in a way that bring clarity to scientific work. For me, the tools by Hadley Wickham and the Tidyverse are a great example of that.

Outside of my scientific work, my hobbies include PC and console gaming, table-top and RPG gaming, and programming. I’m also a keen gardener and animal lover: I currently take care of an Axolotl named Joanna, an African Pygmy Hedgehog named Sonic, and a lovely Romanian rescue dog named, Zelda.
